Competitions

Below is a list of Competitions that are being run at BUC this year. Some competitions require a rider ticket where as others don't. See individual competitions for details

Flatland competition

To enter this competition you must have a rider ticket. This competition will by organised by resident flatland expert Simon Berry. There is NO requirement to wear shinpads or a helmet to compete.

Freestyle competition

To enter this competition you must have a rider ticket. This competition will by organised by resident freestyle expert and champion Sam Goodburn.

Hockey mini tournament

This tournament can be entered with any ticket. This is an informal competition intended to be friendly to beginners. It will be organised by Kris Abraham and Liam McNally.

High Jump competition

To enter this competition you must have a rider ticket. Competitors are required to wear shinpads and a helment to compete.

Street competition

To enter this competition you must have a rider ticket. This competition will by organised by resident street expert and champion Jason Auld. Competitors are required to wear shinpads and a helmet to compete.

Slow Race competition

This competition can be entered with any ticket. There is NO requirement to wear shinpads or a helmet to compete.

Trials competition

To enter this competition you must have a rider ticket. This competition will by organised by resident trials expert Brendan Le Foll.. There will be a beginners and advanced competition. Competitors are required to wear shinpads and a helmet to compete.

Wheel walking competition

This competition can be entered with any ticket. There is NO requirement to wear shinpads or a helmet to compete.

Video competition

We will be holding a video competition at the convention. Accepted videos will be played throughout the weekend (in the lobby) and during a showing on Saturday where convention goers can vote for their favourite. There are a few rules for the videos

  1. The video must involve unicycling.
  2. The must be suitable for younger audiences (i.e. no violence, nudity, sexism, racism or strong language).
  3. The videos length must not exceed 10 minutes.
  4. The video must be uploaded to or already be available on You Tube.
  5. The person submitting the video must be one of the authors of the video.
  6. You may only submit one video.
  7. The deadline for submission is midday, Thursday 23rd of March

If you wish to submit your video please contact committee@unicycle.org.uk giving us your name and a link to the video and we’ll let you know if it’s accepted. Please note the video does not need to be especially for BUC, it can be any unicycle video you’ve made.
